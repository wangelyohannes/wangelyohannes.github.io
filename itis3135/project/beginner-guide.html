Beginner Guide

This guide is designed for users who are interested in anime or manga but do not know where to begin. Instead of dropping straight into the most complex titles, StoryGate encourages users to start with what matches their personal taste.

How to start without getting overwhelmed

  1. Choose a genre you already like in movies, books, or games.
  2. Start with a shorter or more approachable title before tackling huge long-running worlds.
  3. Pay attention to tone. Some stories are uplifting, some are dark, and others are slower and reflective.
  4. Do not assume the most famous title is always the best first title for you.

Recommended Starting Points

If you like action

Start with: Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood or Demon Slayer

These titles are easier starting points because they introduce strong action and clear stakes without expecting a lot of genre knowledge from the viewer.

If you want something emotional

Start with: Your Lie in April or A Silent Voice

These are better for users who care about character emotion and relationships more than constant action.

If you want suspense

Start with: Death Note

This is one of the easiest gateways into thriller anime because the premise is strong, the pacing is clear, and the conflict starts immediately.

If you want something light

Start with: Barakamon or Spy x Family

These are strong beginner titles for users who want charm, humor, and personality without an overly dark tone.

If you like sports or competition

Start with: Haikyuu!!

Sports anime can be one of the easiest entry points because the goals are clear, the energy is high, and the emotional arcs are easy to connect with.

If you want deep worldbuilding later

Try after you are comfortable: One Piece, Bleach, or other longer-form worlds

These can be rewarding, but they are often better once you already know what kinds of stories and pacing you enjoy.

A good beginner rule

Start with the story type you already know you enjoy, not just whatever is most famous. StoryGate is built around the idea that the best first title is the one that matches your taste and keeps you curious enough to continue.
