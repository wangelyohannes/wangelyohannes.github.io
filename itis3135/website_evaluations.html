Good vs Bad Website Evaluations

Zelda Dungeon Wiki - The Legend of Zelda

  • Contrast: The page uses readable text colors against a clean background, and headings stand out clearly from the body content. Links are easy to spot and don’t blend into the paragraph text.
  • Repetition: The wiki layout stays consistent (same heading styles, sidebar navigation, and content formatting). This makes it easy to move through sections without relearning the page layout.
  • Alignment: Content is aligned in a structured way with clear columns and spacing. The page feels organized instead of scattered, which helps you scan for what you need.
  • Proximity: Related items are grouped well (summary info, table of contents, and sections on the same topic). This makes the page feel “chunked” and easier to understand.

Arngren.net

  • Contrast: There’s too much visual noise (images and text layered everywhere), so it’s hard to tell what you’re supposed to read first. Some text gets lost because of the busy background.
  • Repetition: The site doesn’t keep a consistent layout style. Fonts, image sizes, and spacing feel random, so nothing looks like it belongs to the same design system.
  • Alignment: Most elements look like they’re placed without a grid. Items don’t line up, so it feels chaotic and makes the site hard to follow.
  • Proximity: Related content isn’t grouped together. Products, text, and images are scattered across the page, so it’s unclear what goes with what.
